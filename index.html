The challenge

Managing inspections across your authority has never been more complex.

From November 2025, Ofsted's revised Education Inspection Framework replaced single-word grades with multi-area report cards across eleven evaluation areas. For local authorities and MATs, this means more data to manage, more evidence to coordinate, and higher expectations for transparency.

Fragmented data, delayed decisions

Inspection findings sit in spreadsheets, emails, and paper files — making it impossible to see patterns across your schools until it is too late to act.

Rising inspector staff costs

Manual scheduling, travel coordination, and report writing consume inspector capacity. Every hour spent on administration is an hour not spent in schools.

New framework, new evidence demands

The 2025 EIF requires evidence across eleven separate evaluation areas per school. Without a structured system, gathering and collating that evidence is overwhelming.

Accountability without visibility

Boards, elected members, and the DfE expect real-time performance data. Authorities that cannot produce it are exposed in scrutiny meetings and in Ofsted's national data tool.
