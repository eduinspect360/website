The November 2025 EIF changes

What changed — and what it means for your authority.

The November 2025 EIF represents the most significant change to school inspection in England in over a decade. Single-word grade outcomes have been replaced by multi-area report cards across eleven distinct evaluation areas.

Before: Single-word grades

One overall effectiveness grade plus four sub-judgements: Outstanding, Good, Requires Improvement, Inadequate. Simple to communicate but limited in diagnostic value. Schools often focused resources on the single grade outcome rather than specific areas of practice.

After: Multi-area report cards

Eleven evaluation areas, each assessed independently. Far greater transparency and diagnostic depth — but significantly more evidence to coordinate, gather, and report against. Authorities managing inspections without a digital system face a substantial new administrative burden.

Beyond compliance

Configurable for your own quality assurance frameworks.

The Ofsted EIF is the foundation, not the ceiling. EDUINSPECT360 lets authorities build custom frameworks on top — for local authority quality assurance visits, MAT deep dives, governor monitoring, or international inspection programmes.

Local authority QA visits

Configure bespoke framework templates for advisory visits, headteacher reviews, and governor support meetings — distinct from formal Ofsted inspections but using the same evidence-based workflow.

MAT internal assurance

Multi-tier frameworks for MAT quality assurance cycles. Central team oversight with school-by-school evidence tracking and trust-wide trend reporting for the executive and board.

International frameworks

Adaptable to international education inspection models. Custom grade descriptors, multilingual support, and exportable report formats for international deployments beyond the UK.
